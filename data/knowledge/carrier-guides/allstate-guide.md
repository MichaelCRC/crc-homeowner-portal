# CRC Homeowner Guide -- Allstate

## ALLSTATE

### What to Expect
Allstate uses both staff adjusters and independent "ladder assist" vendors for inspections. Their first estimate is often conservative. A second inspection with complete documentation typically produces a more accurate scope.

### How Your Claim Works
1. Allstate assigns an adjuster or ladder assist vendor
2. The inspector climbs the roof and photographs damage
3. Allstate issues an initial estimate
4. CRC reviews and supplements any missing items
5. A re-inspection may be requested with CRC present

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC prepares a complete documentation package before the first adjuster visit
- We request field inspectors rather than staff adjusters when possible
- Every denial or scope reduction gets a written response with specific counter-documentation

### Important: RCV vs ACV
Check your Allstate policy carefully. Some Allstate policies have been converted to ACV (Actual Cash Value) for roofing, which means depreciation is not recoverable. CRC verifies your policy type before work begins so you know your out-of-pocket cost upfront.

### Timeline
Initial inspection: 1-3 weeks
First estimate: 1-2 weeks after inspection
Supplement/re-inspection: 2-4 weeks

### Key Things to Know
- A first denial from Allstate is common and does not mean your claim is invalid
- Complete documentation at re-inspection typically changes the outcome
- If your policy is RCV, depreciation is paid back to you after work is complete
- CRC handles all communication with Allstate on your behalf
