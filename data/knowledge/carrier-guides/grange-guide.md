# CRC Homeowner Guide -- Grange Mutual

## GRANGE MUTUAL

### What to Expect
Grange is headquartered in Columbus and is agent-driven, meaning your insurance agent has real influence over your claim outcome. Grange adjusters are known for writing narrow scopes -- approving one slope or one section when the entire roof needs replacement.

### How Your Claim Works
1. Grange assigns an adjuster (often through an independent adjusting firm)
2. The adjuster inspects and writes a scope
3. If the scope is narrow, CRC immediately invokes Ohio matching law
4. CRC works both the adjuster channel and your agent channel
5. Supplement submissions include matching law citations and documentation

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC checks immediately whether your shingles or siding are discontinued
- If discontinued, Ohio law (OAC 3901-1-54) requires replacement to match -- not partial repair
- CRC works directly with your Grange agent, who has significant influence on claim outcomes
- We prepare ground-level photos showing the mismatch between approved and unapproved areas

### Timeline
Initial inspection: 1-2 weeks
First estimate: 1 week after inspection
Supplement process: 2-4 weeks (may require agent involvement)

### Key Things to Know
- Grange's initial scope is often incomplete -- this is expected, not a denial
- Your Grange agent is your strongest advocate -- CRC coordinates with them directly
- Ohio matching law is the primary tool for expanding narrow Grange scopes
- If your shingles are discontinued, Grange must approve full replacement under Ohio law
