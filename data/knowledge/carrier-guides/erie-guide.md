# CRC Homeowner Guide -- Erie Insurance

## ERIE INSURANCE

### What to Expect
Erie is one of the best carriers to work with in Ohio. They use genuine field adjusters (not ladder assist vendors), and their adjusters are generally thorough and accessible. Erie's Guaranteed Replacement Cost (GRC) coverage is standard on most Ohio policies.

### How Your Claim Works
1. Erie sends a field adjuster to inspect your property
2. The adjuster writes an estimate on-site or shortly after
3. Erie issues payment based on the GRC provision
4. CRC supplements any missing items with IRC code citations
5. Erie is responsive to documented supplements

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC submits a complete scope before the adjuster visit to anchor pricing
- We cite Erie's own GRC policy language in every supplement
- The supplement gap with Erie is typically smaller than other carriers

### Guaranteed Replacement Cost
Erie's GRC means if the actual repair cost exceeds their estimate, Erie pays the full cost. This is your strongest policy benefit. CRC ensures Erie honors this provision on every claim.

### Timeline
Initial inspection: 1-2 weeks
First estimate: within days of inspection
Supplement response: 1-2 weeks (faster than most)

### Key Things to Know
- Erie adjusters are accessible by phone and email
- GRC means you should never pay more than your deductible for covered repairs
- CRC's supplement process with Erie is straightforward and typically resolves quickly
- Erie does not use ladder assist vendors -- you get a real adjuster
