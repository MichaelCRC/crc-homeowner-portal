# CRC Homeowner Guide -- Ohio Homeowner Rights

## OHIO HOMEOWNER RIGHTS

### Your Rights Under Ohio Law

**Right to Choose Your Contractor**
You have the absolute right to choose your own contractor. Your insurance company cannot require you to use their preferred vendor network. CRC works directly with your carrier on your behalf.

**Ohio Matching Law (OAC 3901-1-54)**
When storm damage requires replacement of roofing, siding, or other exterior materials, and the replacement materials cannot match the existing materials in quality, color, or size, your insurance company must replace enough material to achieve a "reasonably comparable appearance." This means if your shingles are discontinued, your carrier cannot do a partial repair that leaves a visible mismatch.

**Right to a Written Denial**
If your insurance company denies any part of your claim, they must provide the reason in writing. A verbal denial is not valid. CRC requests written documentation of every coverage decision.

**Right to Supplement**
If your carrier's initial estimate is incomplete, you have the right to submit additional documentation requesting additional scope. This is called a supplement and is standard industry practice. CRC handles the entire supplement process.

**Right to Appraisal**
If you and your carrier agree that damage exists but disagree on the amount, either party can invoke the appraisal clause in your policy. This brings in a neutral umpire whose decision is binding. CRC can guide you through this process when the gap justifies it.

**Right to File a Complaint**
If your carrier acts in bad faith -- denying legitimate claims without justification, delaying unreasonably, or refusing to provide written reasons -- you can file a complaint with the Ohio Department of Insurance at insurance.ohio.gov.
