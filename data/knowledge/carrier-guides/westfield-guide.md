# CRC Homeowner Guide -- Westfield

## WESTFIELD

### What to Expect
Westfield is an Ohio-headquartered carrier with thorough local adjusters who know Ohio storm patterns well. Their primary risk is classifying legitimate storm damage as "wear and tear." CRC counters this by tying every damage finding directly to a documented storm event.

### How Your Claim Works
1. Westfield sends a local adjuster
2. The adjuster inspects and may question whether damage is storm-related or pre-existing
3. CRC provides NOAA storm data and a storm approach brief proving the storm event
4. Supplements tie every damage finding to the specific storm date and direction

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC documents the storm event with NOAA data before the adjuster arrives
- Our storm approach brief shows wind direction and impact angle for your specific property
- Every damage finding is explicitly tied to the documented storm event
- If Westfield claims wear and tear, CRC provides forensic evidence of storm causation

### Timeline
Initial inspection: 1-2 weeks
First estimate: 1 week after inspection
Supplement response: 2-3 weeks

### Key Things to Know
- Westfield adjusters are thorough -- CRC matches their thoroughness with complete documentation
- Storm causation documentation is critical with Westfield
- CRC's NOAA storm data and approach brief are designed specifically for carriers like Westfield
- Your Westfield agent can be an ally -- CRC coordinates with them when helpful
