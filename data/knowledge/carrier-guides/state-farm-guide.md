# CRC Homeowner Guide -- State Farm

## STATE FARM

### What to Expect
State Farm is the largest home insurer in Ohio. Their claims process uses a combination of field adjusters and desk reviewers. The field adjuster inspects your roof but often does not make the final decision -- a desk supervisor reviews the photos and determines the scope.

### How Your Claim Works
1. State Farm assigns a field adjuster to inspect your property
2. The adjuster photographs the roof and submits findings to a desk reviewer
3. The desk reviewer determines what is approved
4. If approved, State Farm issues payment minus your deductible
5. If the scope is incomplete, CRC submits a supplement with documentation

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC is physically present at every State Farm inspection to ensure all damage is documented
- We verify the Xactimate pricing setting is correct (Restoration/Service/Remodel, not New Construction)
- If State Farm uses their Shingle Locator Service (SLS) to identify your shingles, we verify the result independently
- We submit all code-required items that State Farm commonly omits from initial scopes

### Timeline
Initial inspection: typically within 2-3 weeks of filing
First estimate: 1-2 weeks after inspection
Supplement response: 2-4 weeks after submission

### Key Things to Know
- The field adjuster may not mark damage with chalk -- this is normal for State Farm
- CRC documents everything independently so the desk reviewer has complete evidence
- If your shingles are discontinued, Ohio matching law (OAC 3901-1-54) requires replacement to achieve a comparable appearance
- State Farm supplements often require persistence -- CRC handles the entire process
