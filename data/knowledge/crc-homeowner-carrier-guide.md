## UNIVERSAL CLAIM PROCESS

Every insurance claim follows the same general steps. Here is what happens and what CRC handles for you at each stage.

### Stage 1: Inspection and Documentation
CRC inspects your property and documents all storm damage using professional photography, measurements, and industry-standard protocols. We use CompanyCam for photo documentation and Hover for precise roof measurements. This documentation package is built to the same standard insurance adjusters use.

### Stage 2: Scope and Estimate
CRC generates a complete repair scope using Xactimate, the same software your insurance company uses. Our scope includes every legitimate line item required by Ohio building code, including items carriers frequently omit. We use current OHCO8X pricing for the Columbus market.

### Stage 3: Insurance Review
Your insurance company sends an adjuster to inspect the property. CRC coordinates with your adjuster and is present at the inspection whenever possible. The adjuster writes their own estimate and compares it to ours.

### Stage 4: Supplement (if needed)
If the insurance company's estimate is lower than the actual repair cost, CRC submits a formal supplement request documenting every missing or under-scoped item. This is the most common step -- most initial carrier estimates require supplementing.

### Stage 5: Approval and Scheduling
Once the insurance company approves the full scope, CRC schedules your repair. RCV (Replacement Cost Value) policies pay the approved amount minus your deductible upfront, with recoverable depreciation paid after work is complete.

### Stage 6: Completion and Final Payment
CRC completes the work, you inspect and approve, and any remaining depreciation is submitted to your carrier for recovery.

### Your Role
- Be available for the adjuster inspection (CRC will be there too)
- Sign the authorization for CRC to communicate with your carrier
- Review and approve the final scope before work begins
- Inspect completed work
- You do not need to negotiate with your insurance company -- that is what CRC does

## STATE FARM

### What to Expect
State Farm is the largest home insurer in Ohio. Their claims process uses a combination of field adjusters and desk reviewers. The field adjuster inspects your roof but often does not make the final decision -- a desk supervisor reviews the photos and determines the scope.

### How Your Claim Works
1. State Farm assigns a field adjuster to inspect your property
2. The adjuster photographs the roof and submits findings to a desk reviewer
3. The desk reviewer determines what is approved
4. If approved, State Farm issues payment minus your deductible
5. If the scope is incomplete, CRC submits a supplement with documentation

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC is physically present at every State Farm inspection to ensure all damage is documented
- We verify the Xactimate pricing setting is correct (Restoration/Service/Remodel, not New Construction)
- If State Farm uses their Shingle Locator Service (SLS) to identify your shingles, we verify the result independently
- We submit all code-required items that State Farm commonly omits from initial scopes

### Timeline
Initial inspection: typically within 2-3 weeks of filing
First estimate: 1-2 weeks after inspection
Supplement response: 2-4 weeks after submission

### Key Things to Know
- The field adjuster may not mark damage with chalk -- this is normal for State Farm
- CRC documents everything independently so the desk reviewer has complete evidence
- If your shingles are discontinued, Ohio matching law (OAC 3901-1-54) requires replacement to achieve a comparable appearance
- State Farm supplements often require persistence -- CRC handles the entire process

## ALLSTATE

### What to Expect
Allstate uses both staff adjusters and independent "ladder assist" vendors for inspections. Their first estimate is often conservative. A second inspection with complete documentation typically produces a more accurate scope.

### How Your Claim Works
1. Allstate assigns an adjuster or ladder assist vendor
2. The inspector climbs the roof and photographs damage
3. Allstate issues an initial estimate
4. CRC reviews and supplements any missing items
5. A re-inspection may be requested with CRC present

### What CRC Does for You
- CRC prepares a complete documentation package before the first adjuster visit
- We request field inspectors rather than staff adjusters when possible
- Every denial or scope reduction gets a written response with specific counter-documentation

### Important: RCV vs ACV
Check your Allstate policy carefully. Some Allstate policies have been converted to ACV (Actual Cash Value) for roofing, which means depreciation is not recoverable. CRC verifies your policy type before work begins so you know your out-of-pocket cost upfront.

### Timeline
Initial inspection: 1-3 weeks
First estimate: 1-2 weeks after inspection
Supplement/re-inspection: 2-4 weeks

### Key Things to Know
- A first denial from Allstate is common and does not mean your claim is invalid
- Complete documentation at re-inspection typically changes the outcome
- If your policy is RCV, depreciation is paid back to you after work is complete
- CRC handles all communication with Allstate on your behalf

Erie's GRC means if the actual repair cost exceeds their estimate, Erie pays the full cost. This is your strongest policy benefit. CRC ensures Erie honors this provision on every claim.

- CRC's supplement process with Erie is straightforward and typically resolves quickly
- Erie does not use ladder assist vendors -- you get a real adjuster

## OHIO HOMEOWNER RIGHTS

### Your Rights Under Ohio Law

**Right to Choose Your Contractor**
You have the absolute right to choose your own contractor. Your insurance company cannot require you to use their preferred vendor network. CRC works directly with your carrier on your behalf.

**Ohio Matching Law (OAC 3901-1-54)**
When storm damage requires replacement of roofing, siding, or other exterior materials, and the replacement materials cannot match the existing materials in quality, color, or size, your insurance company must replace enough material to achieve a "reasonably comparable appearance." This means if your shingles are discontinued, your carrier cannot do a partial repair that leaves a visible mismatch.

**Right to a Written Denial**
If your insurance company denies any part of your claim, they must provide the reason in writing. A verbal denial is not valid. CRC requests written documentation of every coverage decision.

**Right to Supplement**
If your carrier's initial estimate is incomplete, you have the right to submit additional documentation requesting additional scope. This is called a supplement and is standard industry practice. CRC handles the entire supplement process.

**Right to Appraisal**
If you and your carrier agree that damage exists but disagree on the amount, either party can invoke the appraisal clause in your policy. This brings in a neutral umpire whose decision is binding. CRC can guide you through this process when the gap justifies it.

**Right to File a Complaint**
If your carrier acts in bad faith -- denying legitimate claims without justification, delaying unreasonably, or refusing to provide written reasons -- you can file a complaint with the Ohio Department of Insurance at insurance.ohio.gov.
